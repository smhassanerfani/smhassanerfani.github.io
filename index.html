Biography

Mohammad H. Erfani is a postdoctoral research scientist at NASA's Goddard Institute for Space Studies (GISS) and the Center for Climate Systems Research (CCSR) at Columbia University. His research focuses on applying machine learning to hydrologic and Earth system models. Before joining CCSR, he conducted research on flood detection and early warning systems using computer vision and machine learning. Mohammad holds a Ph.D. in Civil and Environmental Engineering, with a specialization in Water Resources, from the University of South Carolina.

Research Interests

  • Climate Change and Hydroclimatic Extremes Modeling
  • Machine Learning for Earth System and Climate Modeling
  • Physics-Informed Machine Learning for Geophysical Systems
  • Stochastic and Probabilistic Approaches to Environmental Modeling
